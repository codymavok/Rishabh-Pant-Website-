A timeline of Rishabh Pant

  • October 2015Rishabh Pant makes his first class debut on 22nd October 2015 in the 2015-16 Ranji Trophy.
  • December 2015Rishabh Pant makes his List A debut on 23rd December 2015 in the 2015-16 Vijay Hazare Trophy.
  • Febuary 2016Rishabh Pant is bought by Delhi Daredevils for 1.9 crore in the IPL Auction. On the same day be scores an important century for India in the U-19 World cup to guide India to the Semi-Finals.
  • April 2016In only his third IPL match, Pant scores 69 of 40 balls to guide Delhi Daredevils to victory over Gujurat Lions.
  • October 2016Rishabh Pants blasts 308 runs of just 328 balls v Maharashtra in the Ranji Trophy. He became the third-youngest Indian to score a triple century in first class cricket.
  • November 2016On the 8th of November Pant scored the fasted century in the Ranji Trophy. It just took him 48 balls to achieve this feat in Delhi's match v Jharkhand.
  • February 2017Pant is named the Captain for Delhi in the 2016-2017 Vijay-Hazare Trophy, taking over from Gautam Gambhir.
  • February 2017Pant made his debut for India on February 1st 2017 v England in a T20i match. Pant was the youngest player to debut for India in a T20i match at the time.
  • May 2017Pant blasts 97 runs of just 43 balls in an IPL match on May 4th v Gujurat Lions.
  • January 2018On January 14th, In a Zonal T20 League fixture between Himachal Pradesh and Delhi, Pant scored the second fastest T20 century scoring 100 of just 32 balls.
  • May 2018Pant scores 128 not out from 63 balls v Sunrisers Hyderabad. The highest individual score by an Indian Cricketer in the IPL at the time.
  • August 2018Pant makes his test debut for India against England.
  • September 2018Pant scores his maiden International century against England in a test match. He became the second-youngest wicket-keeper and the first Indian wicket-keeper to score a test century in England.
  • October 2018Pant makes his ODI debut for India on the 21st of October against the West Indies.
  • December 2018Pant took eleven catches in a Test match v Australia, the most by a Wicket-Keeper in a test match.
  • Janary 2019Pant became the first wicket-keeper for India to score a century in a Test match against Australia.
  • June 2019Pant was called up to the India World Cup squad as a replacement for the injured Shikhar Dhawan. Post the world cup ICC named Pant the rising star of the Indian team.
  • September 2019During the second test v the West Indies, Pant became the fastest wicket-keeper for India to affect 50 dissmisals.
  • January 2021During the third test match against Austrlaia, Pant played a career changing knock. With 97 overs to survive on the last day, things did not start well for India as they lost the wicket of Ajinkya Rahane, who had scored a century in the last test, early in the day. With Jadeja having fractured his arm earlier in the match, India were also a batsman short. Pant was promoted up the order to number 5. He hit 12 fours and 3 sixes in his counter-attacking knock of 97 runs off just 118 balls. His 148-run partnership with Pujara put India in a winning position. The match ultimetly ended in a historic draw.
  • January 2021On the fourth and final test againt Austrlia, India were going into the test as underdogs. Australia had not lost a match at the Gabba since 1988. Furthermore, India were missing multiple key players such as Virat Kohli, Jaspirt Bumrah, Mohammed Shami, Ravichandaran Ashwin, Ravindra Jadeja, Hanuma Vihari, Umesh Yadav and Ishant Sharma. Pant played an game changing innigs of a lifetime, as India chased down 328 runs in the 4th innings. Pant scored an unbeaten 89 with 9 fours and a six. He was awarded man of the math for such a brilliant performance.
  • March 2021In March 2021, Rishabh Pant was named the captain of the Delhi Capitlas IPL team in the absence of Shreyas Iyer, who was ruled out of the first half of the IPL after dislocating his shoulder v England in an ODI match.
  • April 2021Pant led Delhi Capitals for the first time v Chennai Super Kings. He became the 4th youngest Indian and 5th youngest overall to captain an IPL side. He succesfully led Delhi Capitals to victory in thier first match as they beat Chennai Super Kings by 7 wickets.
